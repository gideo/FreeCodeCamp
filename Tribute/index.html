Alan Turing

The Father of Modern Computing

British scientist, mathematician, and logician Alan Turing shaped modern computing and broke the Nazi Enigma machine.


    Early Life

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1926 - Went to Sherborn School in Sherborne, Dorset. An atheletic personality, Turing would ride his bicycle 60 miles from Southampton to Sherborne.
  • 1927 - Turing developed a love for mathematics early on, even grasping Einstein's works and Newton's laws of motions. Here he meets his first love, fellow pupil Christopher Morcom.
  • 1930 - Christopher Morcom dies from bovine tuberculosis after drinking infected cow's milk. This affected Turing profoundly, driving him further into science and mathematics- a passion he shared with Morcom.
  • 1931-1934 - Undergraduate at King's College in Cambridge, where he gained first-class honours in mathematics.
  • 1936-1938 - Studied under Alonzo Church at Princeton University, after improving upon Church's lambda calculus proof ultimately becoming the Church-Turing thesis. He obtained his PhD from Princton and wrote his dissertation on the concept of ordinal logic and relative computing.

    • World War II

  • World War II - Turing joins the war efforts in Bletchley Park, a wartime station dealing with cryptoanalysis.
  • 1941 - Porposed to Hut 8 colleague Joan Clarke but later decided not to go through with it.
  • 1942 - Traveled to the United States and worked with US cryptanalysts. During his trip he also assisted Bell Labs with the development of secure speech devices.
  • 1941 - Along with cryptanalyst Hugh Alexander, Stuart Milner-Barry, and Gordon Welchman they work on breaking the Eniga machina by building upon the works of the Polish's cryptologic bomb. However the task proved difficult
  • 1945 - Award the OBE by King George VI although his contributions remained undisclosed for many years.

    • Post-War

  • 1948 - Turing worked with his former colleague D. G. Champerowne to write a chess program that would be completed in 1950. He also invents the LU decomposition method in solving matrix equations.
  • 1952Starts relationship with Arnold Murray, a 19-year-old man. After reporting a burglary, the police questioned Turing where he admits to having sexual relations with Murray. Homosexuality being a criminal offense at the time led to Turing being charged and arrested. In the case Regina v. Turing and Murray, Turing pleads guilty and was forced to undergo chemical castration.
  • 1954 - Died from suicide by cyanide poisoning.
